My gaming history

League of Legends

My first competitve game

My first main competitive game that I got into was League of Legends. This game was most likely what really got me into gaming itself as I spent most of my free time playing this game. I've played this game for about a total of 8 years and my peak rank was Diamond 2 which is part of the top 1%.

Apex Legends

Fun shooter with good movement

Apex Legends was one of those games I enjoyed playing because I was able to showcase my individual skill better. I also really enjoyed the game mechanics, but recently I'm not enjoying the changes the gamedevs have been making towards the game. I've spent about 400 hours on the game and I peaked around Diamond rank in the top 5%.

Dota 2

What I moved to after League of Legends

After I stopped playing League of Legends I went to go find a similar game that felt more enjoyable. I found Dota 2 to be much more complicated than League of Legends, but that was what made it more enjoyable to me. I haven't played too much Dota 2 so far as I've only got about 100-150 hours on the game, and I'm still only mid-rank in the game.

Csgo

Good competitive shooter game

Csgo used to also be a game I played a lot when I would want to take a break from League of Legends. I enjoyed how the game's shooting mechanic worked and mainly how it felt. The pressure I felt from playing made the game seem challenging and fun, but I quit the game because there was a rampant hacker issue. I've spent over 1k hours on the game and reached Legendary Eagle which is just a few ranks away from the top.

Influence

How gaming has shaped me

If someone were to ask me how was my childhood, it wouldn't be wrong to say that gaming was my childhood. Most of the memories I'm fond of are of me playing video games, but I wouldn't really say it was a bad thing. Video games taught me early on that nothing was free and I'd have to work towards something I wanted. They also taught me that hardwork is important, but being efficient is just as important. There are a multitude of things that I've learned from games, both good and bad, but I think the initial experiences helped develop my character. Gaming has exposed me to different viewpoints or scenarios I would've never thought about by myself, and that has allowed me to become the person I am today.
